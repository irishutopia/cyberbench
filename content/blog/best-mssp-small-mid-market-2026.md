# Best Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) for Small & Mid-Market Business (2026)

Most small and mid-market businesses can't staff a 24/7 security operations team — and attackers know it. A managed security service provider (MSSP) or MDR (Managed Detection and Response) partner fills that gap, delivering round-the-clock monitoring and response for a predictable monthly fee.

This guide compares leading options for smaller organizations and clarifies the choice buyers wrestle with most: MSSP vs. MDR.

## MSSP vs. MDR: which do you actually need?

- **MSSP** — manages a broad set of security services: 24/7 monitoring, firewall and device management, GRC/compliance support.
- **MDR** — focuses specifically on detecting and *actively responding* to threats, usually with a stronger emphasis on speed and outcomes.

For many small businesses in 2026, **MDR is the more proactive, recommended starting point** — you get detection *and* response rather than just alerts in a queue.

## What to evaluate (buyer criteria)

1. **24/7 SOC coverage** — is it truly around the clock, with humans?
2. **Response, not just alerting** — do they act, or just notify you?
3. **Coverage scope** — endpoints, cloud, identity, network; what's in vs. out.
4. **Compliance support** — do they help with your frameworks (SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI, CMMC)?
5. **Transparent pricing & contract terms** — month-to-month vs. multi-year lock-in.

## The providers (2026)

- **Trustwave (LevelBlue)** — One of the world's largest pure-play MSSPs; full suite from firewall management and MDR to GRC consulting. Pricing is quote-based and typically aligned to multi-year contracts rather than low-budget SMB tiers.
- **Arctic Wolf** — Cloud-native security operations platform delivering managed detection and response through a named "concierge" security team. A widely recognized mid-market choice for organizations that want broad managed coverage without building their own SOC.
- **Huntress** — Managed detection and response built for small and mid-market businesses (and the MSPs that serve them), backed by a 24/7 SOC. A strong entry point for smaller teams that want real human response on a predictable budget.
- **Gradient Cyber** — Tailored solutions for small and mid-market enterprises, with 24/7 monitoring and response.
- **Avertium** — Mid-market and enterprise focus; managed extended detection and response (MXDR), actionable threat intelligence, and attack surface reduction.
- **Foresite** — MDR services combining advanced technology and human expertise to detect and respond to threats in real time.
- **VISO Group / ThreatScope** — Continuous external attack surface management and threat validation that *complements* an MDR/MSSP by showing what's exposed before attackers find it. A fit for mid-market teams hardening their external footprint. *(Disclosure: VISO Group operates CyberBench.)*

## Pricing reality (2026)

| Segment | Typical monthly cost |
|---------|---------------------|
| Small business | $1,500–$8,000 |
| Mid-sized organization | $5,000–$20,000 |
| Enterprise | $20,000–$100,000+ |

Costs scale with employee count, number of endpoints, and coverage scope. Many SMB and mid-market MSSP/MDR deals are priced per user (commonly ~$20–$250 per user per month depending on scope and add-ons like EDR licenses and incident-response retainers).

*Note: the MDR market continues to consolidate — Red Canary now operates under Zscaler (acquisition completed August 2025) — so confirm current ownership and SLAs when you shortlist.*

## Frequently asked questions

**What is an MSSP?**
A managed security service provider delivers outsourced security operations — 24/7 monitoring, threat detection and response, firewall management, compliance support — so you don't have to build a full in-house team.

**How much does an MSSP cost for a small or mid-market business in 2026?**
Small businesses typically pay $1,500–$8,000/month and mid-sized organizations $5,000–$20,000/month, depending on employee count, endpoints, and scope. Enterprise programs can exceed $100,000/month.

**What's the difference between MSSP and MDR?**
An MSSP manages a broad set of services; MDR focuses on detecting and actively responding to threats. For many small businesses, MDR is the more proactive starting point.

**Do small businesses really need an MSSP?**
Most lack a 24/7 in-house security team — exactly the gap an MSSP or MDR fills, usually far cheaper than building an internal SOC.
